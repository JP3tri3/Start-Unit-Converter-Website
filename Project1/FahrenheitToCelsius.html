Formula:

(F-30)/2 = Celsius

History:

The Fahrenheit scale is a temperature scale based on one proposed in 1724 by the physicist Daniel Gabriel Fahrenheit (1686–1736). It uses the degree Fahrenheit (symbol: °F) as the unit. He noted a middle point of 32 °F, to be set to the temperature of ice water.

The degree Celsius is a unit of temperature on the Cesius scale, a temperature unit scale originally known as the centigrade scale. It is named after the Swedish astronomer Anders Celsius (1701–1744), who developed a similar temperature scale. Since 1743 the Celsius scale has been based on 0 °C for the freezing point of water and 100 °C for the boiling point of water at 1 atm pressure.
